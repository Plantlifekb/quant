Quant v1.0 — Signal Stability Specification

Canonical, governed, non‑drifting

Purpose

Define the rules, invariants, and deterministic checks that ensure Quant v1.0 signals remain stable, interpretable, and free from noise‑driven volatility. This protects the strategy from erratic behaviour and ensures that score‑based rankings reflect genuine structural information rather than transient fluctuations.

1. Stability Philosophy

Quant v1.0 enforces:

deterministic signals

no smoothing

no randomness

no hidden transformations

no adaptive weighting

no regime‑dependent adjustments

Signal stability is achieved through clean inputs, simple transformations, and strict invariants, not through smoothing or filtering.

2. Inputs

2.1 Required Fields

From enriched_daily.csv:

ret_1d

ret_20d

vol_20d

score

mkt_trend

From weekly_top10_quant_v1.csv:

rank

ticker

3. Stability Rules

3.1 No Smoothing

Forbidden:

exponential moving averages

weighted moving averages

rolling medians

Kalman filters

low‑pass filters

3.2 No Winsorization or Clipping

All raw values must remain unaltered.

3.3 No Normalization Drift

Cross‑sectional z‑scores must be computed:

once per day

using the full governed universe

with deterministic mean and standard deviation

3.4 No Adaptive Parameters

Forbidden:

volatility‑adjusted weights

regime‑dependent scaling

dynamic lookback windows

3.5 No Randomness

All transformations must be deterministic.

4. Score Stability

4.1 Score Formula Must Not Change

The score is defined as: [ score(t) = 0.5 \cdot ret_{20d}(t) - 0.3 \cdot z(vol_{20d}(t)) + 0.2 \cdot z(ret_{1d}(t)) ]

4.2 No Hidden Adjustments

Forbidden:

score scaling

score normalization beyond z‑scores

score capping

4.3 Daily Score Stability Check

A stability flag is raised if: [ |score(t) - score(t-1)| > 3 \cdot \sigma_{score} ]

This flag is informational only.

5. Ranking Stability

5.1 Deterministic Ranking

Ranking must:

use descending score

break ties alphabetically

produce identical results for identical inputs

5.2 Weekly Rank Stability Check

A stability flag is raised if:

more than 50% of the top 10 change week‑to‑week

This flag is informational only.

6. Portfolio Stability

6.1 Equal Weighting Only

Equal weighting prevents instability from:

score noise

volatility spikes

regime shifts

6.2 No Dynamic Position Sizing

Forbidden:

volatility targeting

risk parity

score‑weighted portfolios

7. Stability Invariants

No smoothing

No clipping

No randomness

No adaptive parameters

No external data

No hidden transformations

No dynamic weighting

No regime‑dependent scaling

8. Validation

8.1 Schema Validator

Ensures all stability‑related fields exist.

8.2 Compliance Checker

Ensures no forbidden transformations are used.

8.3 Directory Auditor

Ensures no alternative signal files exist.

9. Failure Handling

If stability checks fail:

pipeline continues (informational only)

stability flags are logged

operator reviews during weekly audit

If forbidden transformations are detected:

pipeline stops

incident must be declared

End of Signal Stability Specification
